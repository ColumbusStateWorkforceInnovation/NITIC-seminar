# The Storytime Playbook: "Admiral Bash's Island Adventure"

This document contains the exact text selections from the CNCF book *Admiral Bash's Island Adventure*. Use these 1-2 minute readings at the beginning and end of each day to ground the technical labs in the overarching Cloud Native narrative.

*Instructor Note: It is highly recommended to display the PDF of the book on the projector during these readings so the students can see the illustrations.*

---

## Day 1: The Ship Has Sunk

### Morning Reading (9:00 AM)
*Context: Read this immediately after the welcome remarks to explain why we are abandoning monolithic VMs for the class.*

> "A long time ago, Admiral Bash commanded the oceans... His ship, the SS Legacy, was built around a mainframe which made it slow to change course. Suddenly, out of nowhere, it was attacked by pirates! The heavy mainframe fell through the hull and dragged the ship to the bottom of the ocean."

### Afternoon Reading (4:50 PM)
*Context: Read this to close out Day 1, summarizing the transition from the heavy OS to small, modular Containers (Rafts).*

> "Admiral Bash knew it was time for a new approach. The old, slow ways of working were no more. Washed ashore on an uninhabited island, he and his crew had to rebuild from scratch. With the help of the penguins, they broke the work down into smaller problems, creating tiny, easily replaceable tools and building supplies."

---

## Day 2: Meet the Crew

### Morning Reading (9:00 AM)
*Context: Read this to open Day 2. It perfectly introduces Kubernetes Deployments and the shift to using `k9s` to monitor the chaos.*

> "As everything grew larger and larger, the island became more chaotic. They needed someone to help organize the settlement. Luckily, Captain Kube was happy to help. With Captain Kube guiding them, the penguins knew exactly what they should be doing at any given moment."

### Afternoon Reading (4:50 PM)
*Context: Read this to close out Day 2, reflecting on the 3-Tier Fleet Logistics lab where students connected Services across namespaces.*

> "The penguins needed a way to better connect their distinct settlements. Linky arrived with lots of strong and secure ropes! Linky created dedicated and direct communication routes between the settlements, bypassing the unreliable jungle paths."

---

## Day 3: Automated Shipyards

### Morning Reading (9:00 AM)
*Context: Read this to introduce Helm templates and infrastructure automation.*

> "Before they knew it, the settlements had grown into a thriving metropolis. The beavers had built a complex infrastructure of dams, pipes, and canals to supply the city. It was highly automated, and everyone relied on it."

### Afternoon Reading (4:50 PM)
*Context: Read this to segue into tomorrow's GitOps (ArgoCD) concepts, emphasizing the need to enforce the "truth" of the repository.*

> "However, the peace was soon broken by a group of Regulator Ravens. They demanded the islanders produce documentation, procedures, and strict rules for how things were built. Zee helped everyone document everything properly, ensuring no unauthorized changes could happen without approval."

---

## Day 4: The Admiral's Challenge

### Morning Reading (9:00 AM)
*Context: Read this directly before the Chaos Mesh live-fire exercise where we break the students' clusters.*

> "One day, the pirates returned! They snuck a spy onto the island to cause chaos and disrupt the water supply. But the island was ready. The beavers had built redundancy into the dams, and the Regulator Ravens’ strict policies meant the pirate's unauthorized packages were instantly rejected."

### Afternoon Reading (4:50 PM)
*Context: The final reading to close the seminar. A perfect summary of the Cloud Native Maturity Model.*

> "Goldie was pleased: 'Our applications match our platforms.' The old mainframe monoliths had been broken up, and applications were now loosely coupled. The crew was able to update parts of the applications much more easily. Admiral Bash exclaimed: 'This is what we wanted! We’ve come so far since the pirates sunk our mainframe, and we settled this uninhabited island.' With that, a cheer went up! Hooray!"
